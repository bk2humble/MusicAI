✅ What's Working Well

Your drum pattern has excellent pocket and groove. The snare placement on beat 2 and 4 creates a natural head-nod feel that suits the genre perfectly.
The chord progression has a strong emotional identity — the minor 6th movement in the chorus creates tension that resolves beautifully. Keep this.
Your vocal delivery in the hook has authentic emotion. The slight pitch wavering on held notes gives it a raw, relatable quality.

⚡ Areas to Improve

Your low-end is muddy between 60–120Hz. There's frequency clash between the kick drum and bass guitar. Apply a high-pass filter on the bass at 80Hz and add a 3–4 dB cut on the kick around 100Hz to create separation.
The verse feels too long relative to the chorus — currently 32 bars vs 8 bars in the hook. Consider trimming the 2nd verse by 8 bars or adding a pre-chorus to build anticipation.
The hi-hat pattern becomes monotonous in the second half. Introduce open hi-hats on the "and" of beat 2 every 4 bars and add subtle ghost notes to create texture without overwhelming the groove.

🎛️ Recommended Loops & Instruments

🥁
808 Sub Bass Layer

Add a pitched 808 underneath your bass for sub-frequency power. Tune to the root note of each chord. Recommended: Splice "Dark 808 Pack" or KSHMR 808s.

🎹
Rhodes Electric Piano

A soft Rhodes chord stab on the "and" of beat 3 would fill the mid-frequency gap in your arrangement. Use Keyscape or a free Kontakt Rhodes patch.

🎺
Brass Section Accent

A short brass stab at the end of your chorus phrase would add commercial punch. Try Heavyocity Forzo or a free Spitfire LABS brass patch.

🎸
Finger-Picked Guitar Loop

Layer a subtle acoustic guitar loop at –12 dB underneath your beat to add organic warmth and depth. BPM-synced to your track (current: 94 BPM).
